# How To Capture Tridolon

All Eidolons follow the same basic mechanic: you must first remove their shield with your AMP before you can damage their limbs.

## Breaking Eidolon Synovias (Limbs)

{% hint style="warning" %}
Before destroying a limb, you need a [<mark style="color:$success;">**charged Eidolon Lure**</mark>](../lure-management-basic-info.md).
{% endhint %}

Once your lure is charged, you can safely destroy one of the Eidolon’s synovias.

When a synovia is destroyed, the Eidolon will kneel for about **17 seconds**. Shortly after the Eidolon will release 4 magnetic waves, each applying the [magnetic status effect](https://wiki.warframe.com/w/Damage/Magnetic_Damage).

Stay in [**Void Mode**](https://wiki.warframe.com/w/Void_Mode) on your Operator to avoid taking damage.

Magnetic effects can be completely negated using an arcane included in all builds: [**Arcane Nullifier**](https://wiki.warframe.com/w/Arcane_Nullifier) (+102% magnetic status resistance).

{% hint style="danger" %}
If a charged lure is not close to the Eidolon when it falls, the Eidolon will teleport to a random location
{% endhint %}

This cycle is repeated:

* 4 times for Teralyst
* 6 times for Gantulyst and Hydrolyst

#### Healing Phase

After all synovias are destroyed, the Eidolon enters a **Healing Phase**, lasting about **48 seconds**. During this time, it summons Vomvalysts to heal itself.&#x20;

As it falls to the ground, it spawns **3–4 Vomvalysts** in intervals of **2–3 seconds**

#### Capshot

When the Eidolon stands back up, you can perform a Capshot: this is where you deplete the final portion of its health bar, making it vulnerable anywhere on its body for the capture.

{% hint style="info" %}
As it rises, it will spawn additional Vomvalysts if needed, usually 4–6 in total.
{% endhint %}
